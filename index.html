Does your home qualify?

Solar usually makes the most sense when the basics line up.

A quick review usually comes down to a few practical factors. These do not guarantee that solar is right for your home, but they are some of the first things worth checking.

Roof condition

A roof in decent shape usually gives you more flexibility and fewer surprises.

Sun exposure

Tree coverage, roof direction, and usable layout can all affect production and value.

Electric bill size

Homes with moderate to higher bills tend to have more room for meaningful savings.

Credit profile

Many solar options simply require decent to good credit, especially when the goal is lower upfront cost.

Questions homeowners ask first

Short answers before you go deeper.

Will solar actually lower my bill?

It can, but the answer depends on your home, your usage, your roof, and the type of solar option that fits your situation.

Do I need a perfect roof?

No, but roof age, usable space, and sunlight all matter. A review can tell you quickly whether those basics line up.

Does solar always require financing?

No. Many homeowners explore lease-style options or other lower-upfront-cost paths, but credit still usually matters.
